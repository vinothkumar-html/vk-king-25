A paragraph should revolve around a central theme or idea, ensuring coherence and focus. Sentences: It comprises multiple sentences that develop, explain, or support the main idea. Structure: Paragraphs are typically indented or separated by a line break to visually distinguish them from other paragraphs. Purpose: Paragraphs help to organize information, making it easier for readers to grasp the main points and follow the flow of the text. Topic Sentence: A well-organized paragraph often begins with a topic sentence that introduces the main idea of the paragraph. Supporting Details: The sentences following the topic sentence provide supporting details, examples, or evidence to elaborate on the main idea. Coherence and Unity: All sentences within a paragraph should be related to the central idea, creating a sense of unity and flow.
